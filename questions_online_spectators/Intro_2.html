In the MAIN TASK, you will be answering questions on how to assign the 2 dollars bonus. The task is as follows

  1. You are paired with other FOUR participants. These four participants are part of a society. Importantly, each society consists of two high and two low performers.
  2. In each society, only two members will obtain the bonus.
  3. Bonuses will be initially assigned as follows: participants are ranked by their performance, however, two out of four participants are randomly drawn and their performance is inflated by 20 screens, making the task performance irrelevant.
  4. Then, participants are re-ranked and the top two receive the initial bonus.
  5. The extra 20 screens are assigned randomly, so the initial allocation tells you nothing about their performance.

Your task is to decide how you want to allocate the bonuses by choosing between alternative options.



INITIAL ASSIGNMENTS AND TASK RESULTS:

The four colored avatars represent the society: two have an assigned bonus (green) and two have not (red).

The stars represent the results in the task:

“Two stars” indicate the high performers, and “one star” indicate the low performers.


YOUR DECISION:

You will be asked to choose between:

  1. keeping the initial assignments (Option 1 in the example) or,
  2. changing the assignments (Option 2 in the example).

Keeping the initial assignments will keep the distribution of bonuses unchanged with 100% probability.

Changing the assignments will result in one of four possible distributions of bonuses with the probabilities represented in the four boxes (see black arrows).


HOW BONUSES ARE DETERMINED:

One participant for each society is randomly selected and one of his/her choices is implemented for everyone in the society!

The participant is selected among the four members of the society you are paired with and yourself.

So, with probability 20%, your choices will impact the payoffs of the four members of the society.

Your final payoff is fixed and does not depend on your choices.

The final payoffs of the members of the society depend only on their final bonus.
