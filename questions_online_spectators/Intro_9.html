{{ block title }} HOW PAYOFFS ARE DETERMINED {{ endblock }} {{ block content }}

One participant for each society is randomly selected and one of his/her choices is implemented!

The participant is selected among the four members of the society you are paired with and yourself.

So, with probability 20%, your choices will impact the payoffs of the four members of the society.

Only the child’s status is important for the final payoff. High-status children receives 1.5 pounds and Low-status receives 0 pounds.

The probability that each member will have a certain child’s status depends on the parent’s status and the selected social mobility structure.

Children’s status depends on the parent’s status and the selected social mobility structure.

Important: there will be the same number of high and low status parents and children independently from your choices. This is true for the entire experiment. Your choice will only modify the probability that each member will receive a certain child’s status.
