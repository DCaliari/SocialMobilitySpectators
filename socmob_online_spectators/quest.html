{% if question == "Decision 1" %}

The results of the performed task play no role in determining the payoffs of the members of the society.

The expected payoffs of members with the same parents' status are the same irrespective of their results in the task.

For example, on the left there is No Mobility, so parents and children status are the same.

On the right, there is Full Mobility, and notice that mobility affects equally everyone with the same parent's status.

{% elif question == "Decision 2" %}

The results of the performed task will play a role in determining the payoffs of the members of the society.

If Full Mobility is chosen, the members who had better results in the task will be rewarded.

For example, on the left there is No Mobility, so parents and children status are the same and the results play no role.

On the right, there is Full Mobility, that mobility always reward members with more stars and punish members with less stars.

For example, on the left there is No Mobility, so parents and children status are the same and the results play no role.

On the right, there is Full Mobility, that mobility always reward members with less stars and punish members with more stars.
